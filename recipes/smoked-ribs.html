Smoked Baby Back Ribs

This recipe will help you create a wonderful and delicious pull pork bbq that can be used to make sandwiches, casorols, and many more.

In this recipe I will be giving you my personal spice rub, but you all are more than welcome to alter it to your liking. I will also make sure to give you the instructions on how to prepare, how to cook, and what you need to do after the cook.

Spice Rub

  • 1/2 cup of Paprika
  • 1/2 cup of Salt
  • 1/2 cup of Sugar
  • 1/2 cup of Granulated Garlic
  • 1/4 cup of Granulated Onion
  • 1/4 cup of Chili
  • 1/4 cup of Cumin
  • 2 Tablespoons of Fresh Cracked Peppper
  • 2 Tablesppons of Dry Mustard
  • 1 Tablespoon of Cayenne Pepper

Prep Process, this should be done the day before the cook.

  1. You want to cover your ribs with a binder, I like to use mustard but other use siracha, hot sauce, ect...
  2. After putting the binder on make sure to put your spice rub evenly.
  3. Cover the ribs lightly with plastic wrap and put it in the fridge until cook day.
  4. Pull the ribs out from the fridge about 1 hour before the cook.

Cook Day

  1. Choose your choice of wood, I like to use apple, but any classic wood like hickory or mesquite. Don't be afraid to experiment and try other woods out.
  2. Get your grill up to 250 and place the ribs inside your smoker.
  3. While your ribs is being smoked grab a spray bottle and make sure its clean and fill it up with some liquids. It can be water, beer, apple juice, or apple cider. You can also do a jack and coke as well. The liquid being sprayed on helps with holding on the smoke.
  4. When cooking, you want the final temp to be around 200.
  5. I like to spray about ever 45 mins but after cooking for awhile you will figure out what works best for you.
  6. For me my ribs usually take me about 4 hours to smoke at 250, I cook 2 hours unwraped, and 2 hours wrapped with aluminium.
  7. When wraping I add butter, honey, and brown sugar to help tenderize it.
  8. Once when it reach the 205 temp take it out let it rest for about 30 mins and dig in.
